**Does your mom know how to order food online on her own?**

---

"Why do you always look so frustrated lately?"
"Ugh, it's just... the AI keeps hallucin--- never mind."

That was a real conversation at my house. Explaining AI hallucinations to a parent who still finds smartphones confusing? I didn't even know where to start. I gave up, and felt quietly guilty about it for the rest of the day.

Technology moves fast. The gap between our parents and the rest of the world moves with it.

This blog exists to close that gap --- a little at a time. Whether you're a senior trying to keep up, or an adult child who wants to help but doesn't know where to begin, this is for you.

## Why I Started Writing This

<img src="/images/digital_divide_taxi.png" alt="senior struggling to hail a taxi while younger person uses ride-hailing app on smartphone" style="width:100%; max-height:400px; object-fit:cover; border-radius:12px; margin-bottom:8px;">
<p style="text-align:center; font-style:italic; color:#9E9E9E; font-size:0.9rem; margin-top:0;">Two generations, two completely different experiences of the same moment.</p>

> *"Honey, I accidentally sent that message --- can I delete it?"*
> *"What does this little number mean?"*
> *"Is this the right way to get there? I looked it up myself."*

Sound familiar?

Utility bills moved online almost overnight. Concert and sports venues stopped selling tickets at the door. An older neighbor of mine missed a game he'd followed for decades because he couldn't figure out how to book online. He'd been a fan for years. The venue just assumed everyone had it figured out.

For seniors living alone --- far from family, without someone nearby to ask --- this isn't a minor inconvenience. It's isolation.

I started noticing how many people in my generation were quietly carrying the same stress: fielding the same questions from parents, feeling guilty for getting impatient, not knowing the best way to explain things without making anyone feel small. So I started writing it down.

This isn't a perfect guide. I'm still learning too. But if you landed here after one too many confused phone calls from a parent, I hope something here saves you both a little frustration.
